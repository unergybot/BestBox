# Purchase Order Approval Procedures

## Overview

This document outlines the standard procedures for purchase order (PO) approval within the organization. All purchase requests must follow this workflow to ensure proper financial controls and authorization.

## Approval Process

### Step 1: Initial Request Submission

- Employee submits PO request via ERP system
- Must include: vendor name, item description, quantity, estimated cost
- Attach relevant quotes or proposals (required for purchases over $5,000)

### Step 2: Manager Review

- Department manager reviews request within 2 business days
- Manager verifies:
  - Budget availability in department cost center
  - Business justification and necessity
  - Compliance with procurement policies
- Manager approves or rejects with comments

### Step 3: Financial Review

For purchases over $10,000:
- Finance department conducts additional review
- Verifies vendor status and payment terms
- Confirms budget allocation and GL coding
- May request additional documentation

### Step 4: Executive Approval

Required for purchases over $25,000:
- VP or C-level approval needed
- Strategic alignment review
- Contract terms review for multi-year commitments
- Board approval required for amounts over $100,000

## Important Notes

- Urgent requests (under $1,000) can be processed via expedited workflow
- Recurring vendor purchases may qualify for blanket PO setup
- Always obtain three quotes for purchases over $15,000
- PO numbers must be referenced on all vendor invoices
- Split orders to avoid approval thresholds are prohibited
